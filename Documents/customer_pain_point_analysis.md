# Customer Pain Point Analysis

## Objective

Identify the most frequent and impactful customer issues in order to understand customer pain points and prioritize future AI-driven business improvements.

---

# Analysis Performed

## Complaint Category Distribution

Purpose:

Identify the most common customer complaints and recurring operational issues.

---

## Priority vs Complaint Analysis

Purpose:

Determine which complaint categories generate the highest operational risk.

---

## Queue vs Complaint Analysis

Purpose:

Understand which business functions are responsible for handling customer complaints.

---

## Type vs Complaint Analysis

Purpose:

Determine whether complaints primarily arise as incidents, requests, problems, or change requests.

---

# Key Findings

| Category | Ticket Count |

Security-related issues represent the largest complaint category in the dataset.

* Potential trust and reputation risks.

# High Priority Complaint Analysis

The highest volume of high-priority tickets originates from:

| Category    | High Priority Tickets |
| ----------- | --------------------- |
| Security    | 2,310                 |
| Bug         | 2,251                 |
| Performance | 1,210                 |
| Feedback    | 1,181                 |

### Business Interpretation

Security and Bug issues are both high-frequency and high-priority concerns.

These categories should receive immediate attention due to their potential impact on customer trust, product reliability, and operational efficiency.

# Queue Analysis

The majority of customer complaints are concentrated within the following support functions:

| Queue                | Primary Areas                     |
| -------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Technical Support    | Security, Bugs, Performance       |
| Product Support      | Bugs, Features, Feedback          |
| Customer Service     | Feedback, Features, Security      |
| Billing and Payments | Billing, Payments, Account Issues |

### Business Interpretation

Technical Support and Product Support handle the largest concentration of critical customer issues.

These functions are likely to generate the highest return from AI-enabled automation and support solutions.

# High-Risk Complaint Categories

Complaint categories were prioritized using a weighted risk score based on ticket priority.

Risk Score Formula:

Risk Score =
(High Priority × 2)
+
(Medium Priority × 1)

The highest-risk complaint categories represent the most urgent candidates for process improvement and future AI intervention.

# Conclusion

The analysis indicates that Security, Bugs, and Performance issues represent the most significant customer pain points. Technical Support and Product Support manage the majority of these issues, making them strong candidates for future AI-enabled process improvement initiatives.
