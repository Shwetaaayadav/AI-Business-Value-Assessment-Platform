# KPI Framework

## Overview

This framework defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) used to evaluate the business value of AI initiatives across financial, customer, operational, workforce, and strategic dimensions.

---

# Financial KPIs

## Return on Investment (ROI)

Purpose:
Measure the financial return generated by an AI initiative.

Formula:

ROI = ((Benefits - Costs) / Costs) × 100

Interpretation:

- ROI > 100% → High Value
- ROI 20%–100% → Moderate Value
- ROI < 20% → Low Value

---

## Cost Savings

Purpose:
Measure reduction in operational costs achieved through AI implementation.

Examples:

- Reduced support staff workload
- Reduced manual processing effort
- Reduced operational expenses

---

## Revenue Impact

Purpose:
Measure additional revenue generated through AI systems.

Examples:

- AI Recommendation Systems
- AI Sales Assistants
- Personalized Marketing Engines

---

## Payback Period

Purpose:
Determine the time required to recover investment costs.

Formula:

Payback Period = Total Investment Cost / Annual Benefit

Lower payback periods indicate higher investment attractiveness.

---

# Customer KPIs

## Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT)

Purpose:
Measure customer satisfaction after interacting with AI systems.

Scale:

1 to 5

Interpretation:

- >4 Excellent
- 3–4 Acceptable
- <3 Poor

---

## Resolution Rate

Purpose:
Measure effectiveness of AI support systems.

Formula:

Resolution Rate = Resolved Tickets / Total Tickets

Higher values are better.

---

## Escalation Rate

Purpose:
Measure how often AI systems fail to resolve customer issues.

Formula:

Escalation Rate = Escalated Tickets / Total Tickets

Lower values are better.

---

## Customer Retention Rate

Purpose:
Measure customer loyalty and repeat engagement.

Formula:

Retention Rate = Returning Customers / Total Customers

Higher values indicate stronger customer relationships.

---

# Operational KPIs

## Average Resolution Time

Purpose:
Measure efficiency of customer support processes.

Formula:

Average Resolution Time = Total Resolution Time / Number of Tickets

Lower values indicate greater efficiency.

---

## Time Saved

Purpose:
Measure productivity improvements resulting from AI implementation.

Formula:

Time Saved = Time Before AI - Time After AI

Higher values indicate greater efficiency gains.

---

## Error Reduction

Purpose:
Measure quality improvements.

Formula:

Error Reduction (%) =
((Errors Before AI - Errors After AI) / Errors Before AI) × 100

Higher values indicate improved quality.

---

## Automation Rate

Purpose:
Measure percentage of tasks automated by AI.

Formula:

Automation Rate = Automated Tasks / Total Tasks

Higher values indicate stronger automation capabilities.

---

# Workforce KPIs

## Adoption Rate

Purpose:
Measure employee usage of AI systems.

Formula:

Adoption Rate = Active Users / Eligible Users

Interpretation:

- >70% Strong Adoption
- 40–70% Moderate Adoption
- <40% Weak Adoption

---

## Usage Frequency

Purpose:
Measure how often employees interact with AI systems.

Categories:

- Daily
- Weekly
- Monthly

---

## Productivity Improvement

Purpose:
Measure increase in employee output.

Formula:

Productivity Improvement =
(Output After AI / Output Before AI)

Higher values indicate greater productivity gains.

---

## Employee Satisfaction

Purpose:
Measure employee perception of AI tools.

Scale:

1 to 5

Higher scores indicate greater satisfaction.

---

# Strategic KPIs

## Business Alignment Score

Purpose:
Measure alignment of AI initiatives with organizational goals.

Scale:

1 to 10

---

## Scalability Score

Purpose:
Measure ability to scale AI solutions across departments.

Scale:

1 to 10

---

## Risk Score

Purpose:
Measure operational and implementation risk.

Scale:

1 to 10

Lower values are preferable.

---

## Competitive Advantage Score

Purpose:
Measure strategic differentiation created by AI.

Scale:

1 to 10

Higher values indicate stronger competitive positioning.
