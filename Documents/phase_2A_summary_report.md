# Phase 2A Summary Report

## Objective

The objective of Complaint Categorization was to identify the most frequent customer issues, determine which complaints create the highest operational risk, and understand how complaints are distributed across support functions.

---

# Queue vs Priority Analysis

## High Priority Ticket Distribution

| Queue                           | High Priority Tickets |
| ------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Technical Support               | 4,900                 |
| IT Support                      | 1,677                 |
| Product Support                 | 1,554                 |
| Billing and Payments            | 852                   |
| Service Outages and Maintenance | 815                   |

## Findings

Technical Support handles the largest volume of critical customer issues.

Technical Support alone accounts for nearly three times the number of high-priority tickets handled by Product Support.

IT Support and Product Support also represent operationally significant support functions.

# High-Risk Complaint Analysis

## Risk Score Formula

Risk Score =

(High Priority Tickets × 2)

*

(Medium Priority Tickets × 1)

# Key Business Insights

## Security Issues

Security is the highest-risk complaint category.

Security issues generate both high ticket volume and a large number of high-priority incidents.

Potential impacts include:

* Customer trust reduction
* Regulatory concerns
* Increased operational workload

---

## Product Quality Concerns

Bug-related complaints represent the second-highest risk category.

These issues directly affect product reliability and customer experience.

---

## Performance Issues

Performance-related complaints remain a major contributor to customer dissatisfaction and operational workload.

---

## Product Improvement Signals

Feedback and Feature Request categories collectively account for more than 6,600 tickets.

These categories provide valuable customer intelligence for future product improvements.

---

# Operational Findings

## Technical Support

Primary complaint categories:

* Security
* Bugs
* Performance

This function represents the largest opportunity for future AI-enabled support automation.

---

## Product Support

Primary complaint categories:

* Bugs
* Features
* Feedback

This function represents a strong candidate for AI-powered product intelligence and ticket classification systems.

---

## Billing and Payments

Billing-related complaints are concentrated within a dedicated support function.

This area may benefit from conversational AI and automated support workflows.

---

# Conclusion

The analysis indicates that Security, Bugs, Performance, Feedback, and Feature Requests represent the most important customer pain points.

Technical Support and Product Support manage the majority of these issues and therefore present the strongest opportunities for future AI-driven process improvement initiatives.
